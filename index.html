Perspective

Design that doesn't change an outcome isn't design. It's decoration.

Over 16 years I've lived in the gap between what a business needs and what actually gets built, and closed it. Crypto exchanges. Super-apps. Neobanks. Travel platforms. Regulated banking. Products that millions of people depend on, where the wrong call costs real money and the right one changes a company's trajectory.

I started as a front-end engineer. I know what it costs to build the thing I'm designing. That changes how I design. I scaled the design org at Almosafer from 14 to 25 people. I built Metro Bank's design team from the ground up. I led 11 designers across Careem's super-app. I delivered a neobank MVP at a Kraken that generated $7M in its first eight weeks. I've been the solo IC designing or writing code at midnight and the director in the room where strategy gets set. There's no part of this process I haven't owned, which makes me an influential collaborator.

How I Lead

Let's talk
01

Every decision earns its place

I don't design what looks good in a review. I design what moves numbers in production. If it doesn't trace back to an outcome, it doesn't deliver.

02

I slow down so the team speeds up

Most rework happens because someone skipped alignment. I make sure the room agrees on where we're going before anyone opens Figma. That's how you deliver fast without throwing work away.

03

The org runs after I leave

I've left three companies now. Every design org I built kept delivering at the same standard without me. That's not an accident. It's the whole point. I hire people with taste and judgment, then give them the context to own it.

04

AI is in my workflow. Daily.

Not a talking point on a slide deck. A genuine competitive edge. Faster exploration, better decisions, less wasted time. The taste and judgment are still mine. The speed is multiplied.

Background

Jay
Tulloch

I started building websites at 15. Got a BSc in Product Design from Brunel, then an MBA with a focus on Digital Banking from Cranfield. Somewhere in between, I stopped being a designer who understood business and became a business leader who happens to think in systems, interfaces, and user behaviour.

I've written production code. I've run design teams of 25+. I've presented to exec boards and I've pushed pixels at 2am. Front-end engineer, IC designer, team lead, design director, co-founder. It's the reason I can walk into any room, at any altitude, and add value immediately.

AI is embedded across my entire workflow. The taste is mine. The throughput is multiplied.
